Behaviour

Communication methods, leaving notes

Snow leopards primarily communicate through the use of scents and chemicals, marking overhanging cliffs or trees to ensure another snow leopard does not venture into their vicinity, and also to find partners in the mating season

Marking methods

Marking methods include scraping snow, spraying urine, head rubbing, and clawing trees. They will mark in locations along the travel lines like overhanging rocks and trees, the overhanging rocks serve them well as they protect the scents and chemicals from the snow.

Visual communication

Their long tail is also used to communicate a number of functions one being communicating their current mood. When nearby another Snow Leopard they will emit a chuffing sound as a greeting. Snow leopards will rarely emit a high-pitched yowl to alert other leopards of their location.

Mating behaviour

When females are in heat they will make a yowling sound to attract males. During the mating season the Snow Leopard make sure they are thorough when checking the scent messages to check if they have a mate, they will open their mouth and flare their nostrils while they smell to read it more clearly

mating marking

During the mating season male cats will increase their marking activity 3-5 times the usual so the passing females will mate with them. When the females have selected a partner they will invite the male by spraying a particular scent signifying to the chosen male that he is welcome to approach. Following this the female Snow Leopard will climb to a spot where sound will echo, and they call for hours so the chosen male can find them.
