Feeding

Snow leopards are carnivorous and hunt their own prey. With prey hard to come by they are opportunistic and will take any kind of meat and prey to ensure their survival. They are capable of killing animals three times their weight but usually stick to animals of the Bovidae family including Bhurals, Markhor and Ibex. But when desperate they will eat smaller animals like birds and in the summer will eat marmots.

Hunting

The Bhural are always on the lookout for Snow Leopards and a single miss step or scuttling rock will thwart their chances of a meal, stealth is incredibly important for the Snow Leopards survival as the mountain ranges are ominously silent.

Hunting tactics

The snow leopard will use every advantage they have got to secure a meal, they get as close as possible to the prey and then chase them from above to aid acceleration, with prey as quick and agile as the Bovidae it is not uncommon to end the chase empty handed.

Season of plenty, best time for food

The Bovidae the most important prey to the snow leopard often lose their caution in the mating season where they fight to secure a mate, the males will chase competition away, with steep slopes and Snow Leopards hunting this can be very dangerous for the Bovidae either falling to their death or becoming a leopards meal, as the Bovidae fight a lot during mating season it leaves them too tired to compete with a Snow leopard.

Summer time changes

In the summer when cubs are born the Snow Leopards will follow the Bovidae as they climb to higher altitudes and lower temperatures. As the temperatures rise and the snow melts Marmot rodents emerge from their holes and become prey for the snow leopards. Also during the summer the Bovidae will give birth making easy pickings for the Snow Leopard.

Difficulties of hunting, the incredible mother

When hunting, the mother cannot bring along the cubs for the first few months as hunting in the mountains is so reliant on stealth, with so few prey it is important to succeed. The mother must carry the kills up the cliffs and across large distances to avoid scavengers getting to it before her young.
