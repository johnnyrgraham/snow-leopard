Habitat

“The animals are fighting an elemental battle against hunger and cold” – Silent Roar Snow leopards are widely but sporadically distributed throughout the high mountain ranges of central Asia. 60% of their range occurs in china particularly in Xinjian and Tibet.

Dens for and resting

Steep, rocky and broken terrain is the preferred bedding for Snow Leopards on or nearby mountains. Cliffs and ridges are preferred for daytime resting.

Incredible heights

Snow leopards live in alpine and sub alpine zones from elevations of 900 to 5500 meters or higher.

Following the season

In the winter they will usually migrate to lower altitudes following the prey. Snow leopards generally avoid forest cover and cultivated fields.

Distribution

Snow leopards can be found throughout the high mountain ranges, including the Himalayas and the southern Siberian mountains in Russia. They can be found in the Tibetan plateau and across a range that stretches from china to the mountains of central Asia.

Massive territory

They prefer steep, rugged terrain with rocky outcrops. Where prey can be hard to come by. That is why these carnivores require enormous space to roam, male leopards require up to 200 square kilometres and females require up to 130 square kilometres. In altitudes up to 6 kilometres.
