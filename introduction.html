Introduction

One of the rarest creatures on earth so elusive only few have seen them, named by locals the ghosts of the mountain.

Living in one of the most hostile environments in the world, towering above civilization with unforgiving peaks, rugged terrain in the highest mountains in the world.

The snow leopard, a solitary member of the Panthera Genus that live in the mountain ranges living at altitudes as high as 5500 meters and temperatures as low as -10° Celsius.

The only predators of the cats are humans and other snow leopards competing for resources. Thanks to a Largely Buddhist human population the snow leopards are better off than they could have been.

Conservation

As few as 4500-7000 snow leopards left as the snow leopard prey decline and poachers continue to hunt the cats for eastern medicine and beautiful pelts while almost one snow leopard is killed every day in retaliatory killing after snow leopards attack farmers livestock.

In some places like the Indian Himalayas the local village and the conservation trust have created a livestock insurance to provide the farmers with compensation if an animal is lost to a snow Leopard so the herder does not retaliate
